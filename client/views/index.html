Information

Lunar distance (LD) is as a unit of measure in astronomy. It is the average distance from the center of Earth to the center of the Moon.

The absolute magnitude (H) of an asteroid is a number that is a measure of its brightness as seen by an observer on Earth. The brighter an object appears, the lower its magnitude value (i.e. inverse relation).

Maximum and minimum diameter estimates are based on an asteroid's absolute magnitude. The average estimated diameter (m) was derived by taking the average of those values. In this visualization, 1 pixel represents approximately 1 meter.

Though different concepts, an asteroid's relative velocity (km/s) is depicted alongside the moon's mean orbital velocity (1.022 km/s) purely for visual comparison. In this visualization, the moon and the asteroids will travel approximately 50% of the viewport width, which will represent 100 km. The moon, travelling at about ~1 km/s will take 100 seconds to travel that distance.
